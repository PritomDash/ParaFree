US Resume Format 2026

US Resume Format Guide

American resume standards differ significantly from CVs in Australia, UK, and Canada. Follow these rules to get past ATS filters and impress US hiring managers.

Length
1–2 Pages Maximum

Entry-level: 1 page. 5+ years experience: up to 2 pages. US employers prioritise concise, achievement-focused resumes. Never include everything — tailor each application.

Photo Policy
❌ No Photo — Ever

Photos are strongly discouraged in the USA due to anti-discrimination employment laws (Title VII, ADA). A photo can actually cause your resume to be discarded automatically.

Bullet Points
Action Verb + Metric

Each bullet must start with a strong action verb and include a quantified result. Example: "Increased revenue by 34% by redesigning the onboarding flow for 12,000 users."

ATS Formatting
Plain Text, Standard Fonts

Use standard fonts (Calibri, Arial, Georgia). Avoid tables, columns, headers/footers, images, and text boxes — these break most US ATS systems like Taleo, Workday, and iCIMS.

Address
City, State Only

Never include your full street address. List only "City, State" (e.g. "Austin, TX"). Full addresses flag remote-friendliness concerns and waste valuable resume space.

GPA
Include Only If 3.5+

Include GPA only if it is 3.5 or above AND you graduated within the last 3 years. Once you have 2+ years of work experience, drop the GPA entirely — experience speaks louder.

Should I include a photo on my US resume?+
No. Photos are strongly discouraged in the United States due to anti-discrimination employment laws including Title VII of the Civil Rights Act and the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA). Our templates default to no-photo format for all US applications.
How long should my US resume be?+
Entry-level candidates and recent graduates: 1 page. Professionals with 5 or more years of relevant experience: up to 2 pages maximum. Unlike Australia and the UK where longer resumes are accepted, US employers strongly prefer concise, highly targeted resumes.
What's the difference between a resume and a CV in the USA?+
In the USA, a "resume" is a 1-2 page tailored summary used for most job applications. A "CV" (curriculum vitae) is used exclusively in academia and medicine — it's a comprehensive, multi-page record of your entire career. When applying for most US jobs, you want a resume, not a CV.
