Canadian Resume Format Guide

How to Write a Canadian Resume in 2026

Canadian resume conventions are different from UK CVs and Australian resumes. Here's what works in Canada.

🍁 PGWP Tip: Always State Your Work Authorisation

Add a line under your name and contact details: "Work Authorisation: Post-Graduation Work Permit (PGWP) — Open work permit, valid until [date]"
This immediately clarifies to Canadian employers that you can work without sponsorship. Most international student resumes in Canada skip this — don't make that mistake.

Length
1–2 Pages

Canadian resumes are shorter than Australian (2-3 pages) or UK (2 pages max). New graduates: 1 page. Experienced professionals: 1-2 pages. Never more than 2 pages.

Core Competencies
Include This Section

Canadian resumes typically include a "Core Competencies" or "Key Skills" section near the top — a 2-column list of 8-12 skills. This helps ATS parsing and lets recruiters quickly see your strengths.

Photo
No Photo ✗

Do not include a photo, date of birth, SIN number or marital status on a Canadian resume. This is to prevent discrimination under Canadian human rights law.

Bilingual Skills
Highlight EN/FR

If you speak English and French, list both with proficiency levels (Native, Fluent, Professional, Conversational). Federal government positions and Quebec jobs often require French.

Achievements
Quantify Results

Canadian employers value quantified achievements. Use numbers: "Increased sales by 23%", "Managed team of 12", "Reduced costs by $15K". Vague bullet points are less effective in Canada.

References
Available Upon Request

Do not list references on your Canadian resume. Simply write "References available upon request" if needed, or omit entirely. Most Canadian employers ask for references only after an interview.

Frequently Asked Questions

Canadian Resume Questions Answered

What is the correct resume format in Canada? +
A Canadian resume is typically 1-2 pages long. Include a professional summary, core competencies section, work experience, education and skills. Do not include a photo, date of birth or SIN number. Canadian resumes follow a reverse-chronological format with quantified achievements.
Can I mention my PGWP on a Canadian resume? +
Yes. You should mention your Post-Graduation Work Permit (PGWP) clearly. Add a line: "Work Authorisation: Post-Graduation Work Permit (PGWP) — Open work permit, valid until [date]." This immediately clarifies your eligibility to work for any Canadian employer without requiring sponsorship.
Should I include bilingual skills on my Canadian resume? +
Yes, especially if you are applying in Quebec, for federal government positions or bilingual roles. Clearly state your English and French proficiency levels. Bilingual skills are a significant competitive advantage in Canada's federal public service and many private sector roles.
Is ParaFree good for JobBank Canada applications? +
Yes. ParaFree's ATS-friendly templates are suitable for JobBank.gc.ca, Indeed Canada, LinkedIn and direct applications to Canadian employers. The Classic and Minimal templates are single-column formats that work best for ATS parsing.

Yes. ParaFree is widely used by international students at University of Toronto, UBC, McGill, University of Waterloo, McMaster and other Canadian universities. The builder lets you add PGWP status, international education details and bilingual skills clearly.
Should I include references on a Canadian resume? +
Canadian resumes typically do not list references. Write "References available upon request" at the bottom if you want to acknowledge them, or leave it out entirely. Most Canadian employers ask for references only after a successful interview, not at the application stage.
