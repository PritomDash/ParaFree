Tips for International Students

8 Things International Students Must Put on Their CV

Here's what separates international students who get interviews from those who don't — it's mostly about what's clearly stated vs left ambiguous.

01

State Work Authorisation Clearly

Add a line under your contact info: "Work Authorisation: Student Visa — up to 48 hrs/fortnight" or "PGWP — open work permit valid until [date]". Never leave employers guessing.

02

Highlight Your Language Skills

List every language you speak with proficiency (Native, Fluent, Professional, Conversational). Being multilingual is a real business asset — don't leave it buried at the bottom.

03

Note Your International Education

Always include the country name after your university: "BSc Computer Science, University of Dhaka, Bangladesh (2020–2024)". International degrees can be unfamiliar to local employers.

04

Include Your IELTS or TOEFL

If you scored 7.0+ (IELTS) or 100+ (TOEFL), include it. It proves your English proficiency objectively — more credible than just writing "fluent English". Great for non-native speakers.

05

Match the Local English

Use Australian, British or American English spelling depending on where you're applying. "Organise" vs "Organize", "labour" vs "labor" — small differences that signal local awareness.

06

Quantify International Experience

Frame overseas experience as cross-cultural adaptability: "Led diverse team of 8 across 3 countries", "Adapted operations across Australian and Bangladeshi market contexts". Metrics are powerful.

07

Use a Local Email Address

Create a Gmail address. Avoid using a university email from your home country — it ages badly and can signal you're no longer in the country. A clean Gmail reads as professional and local.

08

LinkedIn Profile Is Mandatory

Add your LinkedIn URL to your CV. International hiring managers Google candidates. A complete LinkedIn profile with a photo significantly increases callback rates vs a bare resume.

Frequently Asked Questions

International Student CV Questions

Do international students need different CVs for each country? +
Yes. CV formats vary significantly by country. UK CVs are called CVs and are 2 pages max. Australian resumes are 2-3 pages. Canadian resumes are 1-2 pages with a Core Competencies section. US resumes are 1 page. Always tailor your CV format to the country where you're applying.
Should I include my student visa on my CV? +
Yes, if you have work rights. State your work authorisation clearly: for Australia "Student Visa 500 — up to 48 hours per fortnight work rights"; for UK "Graduate Route visa — full-time work rights"; for Canada "PGWP — open work permit valid until [date]". This reassures employers about your eligibility and saves everyone time.

Which CV template works best for international students? +
For most countries, the Classic or Modern templates work best. These are clean, ATS-friendly single-column layouts that parse correctly in employer systems. Avoid graphic-heavy templates if applying to corporate, government or graduate scheme roles.

What should international students include that local students don't? +
International students should additionally include: (1) Work authorisation/visa status clearly stated, (2) Language skills with proficiency levels, (3) Cross-cultural experience highlighted as a strength, (4) International education institution with country clearly noted, (5) IELTS or TOEFL scores if strong (7.0+).
How do I explain a visa processing gap on my CV? +
Don't hide gaps. Address them briefly. A short note like "Awaiting visa (applied MM/YYYY)" shows transparency and professionalism. Gap years can be presented as "Language study", "Travel/cultural exchange" or "Internship preparation". Most international employers understand visa timelines.
Should I include my IELTS or TOEFL score on my CV? +
Yes, if your score is strong — IELTS 7.0+ or TOEFL 100+. Include it in your skills or education section: "IELTS: 7.5 (2024)". This provides objective proof of your English proficiency, which is far more credible than simply stating "fluent in English."
