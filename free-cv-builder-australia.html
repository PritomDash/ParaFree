Australian Resume Format Guide

How to Write an Australian Resume in 2026

Australian resume formats differ from the US/UK. Here's exactly what Australian employers expect in 2026.

Length
2–3 Pages

Unlike the US where 1 page is standard, Australian resumes are expected to be 2-3 pages. Recent graduates: 1-2 pages. Senior professionals: up to 3 pages.

Photo
No Photo ✗

Do not include a photo on your Australian resume. It is not expected and can lead to unconscious bias. This is different from European and some Asian countries.

References
2 References Required

Australian employers expect 2 professional references at the end of your resume. Include referee name, title, company and contact number. Avoid "Available on request."

Spelling
Australian English

Use Australian/British English spelling: organisation (not organization), behaviour (not behavior), programme (not program), colour (not color).

Cover Letter
Usually Required

Most Australian job applications require a cover letter. Always include one when applying on SEEK, LinkedIn or directly. ParaFree generates cover letters for free.

Personal Statement
Professional Summary

Include a 3-4 sentence professional summary at the top highlighting your experience, key skills and career goal. Australian recruiters read this first.

International Students in Australia

Resume Tips for International Students in Australia

Building an Australian resume as an international student? Here's what you need to know.

📋

Work Rights Section

Clearly state your visa type and work authorisation. Example: "Student Visa (Subclass 500) — permitted to work 48 hours per fortnight during semester." This reassures employers immediately.

🌏

International Education

List your international degree with the Australian equivalent if possible. Include graduation year, institution name and country. Australian employers are familiar with international qualifications.

💬

Language Skills

List your language proficiency clearly: Native, Fluent, Professional or Conversational. Include IELTS or TOEFL scores if relevant. Bilingualism is a genuine advantage in multicultural Australian workplaces.

🤝

Cross-Cultural Experience

Highlight international experience, cross-cultural teamwork and adaptability. Australian employers in global companies highly value these skills in international candidates.
