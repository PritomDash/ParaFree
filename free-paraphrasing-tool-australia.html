Referencing in Australia

Paraphrasing & Referencing Styles at Australian Universities

Different Australian universities and faculties use different referencing styles. Always check your unit guide — here's what to expect.

Most Common

APA 7th Edition

Used in psychology, health sciences, education and business. Cite paraphrased sources with (Author, Year) in-text. APA 7 is the most widely used referencing style at Australian universities.

Common in Humanities

Harvard (Author-Date)

Popular in arts, social sciences and some business courses. Similar in-text format to APA but with different reference list formatting. Multiple Harvard variants exist — check your uni's specific guide.

Arts & History

Chicago / Turabian

Used in history, fine arts and some humanities courses. Features footnote citations. Chicago 17th edition is current. Less common in Australian universities than APA or Harvard.

Law Schools

AGLC 4 (Australian Law)

Australian Guide to Legal Citation 4th edition. Required in all Australian law schools. Uses footnotes and a different format to other academic styles. Unique to Australian legal education.

Engineering & Science

Vancouver / IEEE

Used in engineering, computing, nursing and biomedical sciences. Numbered in-text citations like [1]. IEEE is standard in computer science and electrical engineering.

Architecture

Chicago Notes & Bibliography

Used in architecture and design disciplines. Features numbered footnotes and a full bibliography. The notes-bibliography variant differs from Chicago Author-Date.
